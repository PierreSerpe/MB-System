25  * mbsys_hs10.h defines the data structures used by MBIO functions
26  * to store data from the Furuno HS-10 multibeam sonar system.
27  * The data formats which are commonly used to store Furuno HS-10
28  * data in files include
29  * MBF_HS10JAMS : MBIO ID 171
30  *
31  * Author: D. W. Caress
32  * Date: December 4, 2000
33  *
34  */
35 /*
36  * Notes on the MBSYS_HSDS data structure:
37  * 1. The Furuno HS-10 multibeam sonar generated 45 beams
38  * of bathymetry and amplitude.
39  * 2. To my knowledge, only one Furuno HS-10 multibeam sonar
40  * has been operated. It was installed on S/V Yokosuka,
41  * a JAMSTEC research vessel. The Furuno HS-10 has since been
42  * replaced by a SeaBeam 2112 multibeam sonar.
43  * 3. A specification for the raw HS-10 data format was
44  * provided by JAMSTEC, and is included below. The raw format
45  * consists of 800 byte binary records in which only the
46  * lower 4 bits of each byte are used.
47  * 4. The actual data files provided to WHOI seem to be simple
48  * 717 byte ASCII records with time, lat, lon, heading,
49  * center beam depth, 45 depths, 45 acrosstrack distances,
50  * 45 beam amplitudes, and a <CR><LF> terminator. Format
51  * 171 supports the actual data we received.
52  * 5. The data received use 5 characters each for depth,
53  * acrosstrack, and amplitude values. Null beams have
54  * depth values of 29999 and acrosstrack values of 99999.
55  * MB-System supports beam flagging by setting flagged
56  * beams negative.
57  * 6. The internal data structure supports the data included
58  * in the format 171 files, and does not yet include values
59  * listed in the raw format spec but not seen in the data
60  * provided.
61  * 7. Comment records are supported as an MB-System extension
62  * where the first two bytes of the record are "##".
63  * Comment records are variable length.
64  * 8. The raw data format specification is as follows:
65  *
66  * ----------------------------------------------------------
67  * HS-10 MNBES Data Format - JAMSTEC
68  *
69  * 800 bytes/record, 10 records/block
70  *
71  * Note: 4 bits from LSB is effective in each byte.
72  * zB. 30 30 35 39 ---> 0 0 5 9 (HEX) = 89 (DEC)
73  * 30 30 32 3D ---> 0 0 2 D (HEX) = 45 (DEC)
74  * The HS-10 processor calculates the water depth by use of
75  * average sound velocity and by correcting the difference
76  * between the true angle of the sound path (obtained by the
77  * true sound velocity profile) and the nominal angle of each
78  * beam (every 2 degrees). The horizontal distance of the n-th
79  * beam is
80  * Distance(n) = Depth(n) * tan[T(n)],
81  * where T(n) is the nominal angle of the n-th beam:
82  * ( T(n) = 2 * (n-23) degrees, n=1,45 ).
83  *
84  * No. Bytes Data
85  * 1. 4 Year
86  * 2. 4 Month
87  * 3. 4 Day
88  * 4. 4 Hour
89  * 5. 4 Minute
90  * 6. 4 Second
91  * 7. 8 Latitude in 1/10000 minute
92  * 8. 8 Longitude in 1/10000 minute
93  * 9. 8 X in 1/10 metre
94  * 10. 8 Y in 1/10 metre
95  * 11. 4 Ship's speed in 1/10 knot
96  * 12. 4 Ship's heading in 1/10 degree
97  * 13. 4x45 45 Water depths in metre
98  * 14. 4x45 45 Intensity of reflection in dB
99  * 15. 4 Selection of navigation
100  * 0:HYB, 1:ANS, 2:MANU(L/L) 3:MANU(X/Y)
101  * 16. 4 Surface sound velocity in 1/10 m/sec
102  * 17. 8 Initial latitude in 1/10000 minute
103  * 18. 8 Initial longitude in 1/10000 minute
104  * 19. 8 Initial X in 1/10 metre
105  * 20. 8 Initial Y in 1/10 metre
106  * 21. 4 Manual bearing in 1/10 degree
107  * 22. 4 Manual ship's speed in 1/10 knot
108  * 23. 4 Ship's draft in 1/10 metre
109  * 24. 4 Offset X in 1/10 metre
110  * 25. 4 Offset Y in 1/10 metre
111  * 26. 4 Selection of sound velocity
112  * 0:no correction, 1:manual input, 2:calculation correction
113  * 27. 4 Average sound velocity in 1/10 m/sec
114  * 28. 4 Input selection of water temperature
115  * 0:AUTO, 1:MANUAL
116  * 29. 4 Water temperature in 1/10 degree
117  * 30. 4 Tide level in 1/10 metre
118  * 31. 4x10 10 Depth of layer in metre
119  * 32. 4x10 10 Temperature of layer in 1/10 degree
120  * 33. 4x10 10 Salinity in 1/10 per mille
121  * 34. 4x10 10 Sound velocity in 1/10 m/sec
122  * 35. 4 Transmitted pulse width
123  * 0:1m, 1:2m, 2:4m, 3:8m
124  * 36. 4 Level of transmission [1-16]
125  * 1:Off, 16:Max, -2dB in each step
126  * 37. 4 Selection of period of tranmission
127  * 0:Auto, 1:Manual
128  * 38: 4 Period of tranmission in second
129  * 39: 4 Pre-amp ATT
130  * 0:OFF, 1:ON
131  * 40: 4 Receiving gain [1-16]
132  * 1:Off, 16:Max, -2dB in each step
133  * 41. 4 TVG [1-4]
134  * 42. 4 AVG [1-4]
135  * 43. 4 Threshold [1-16]
136  * 44. 4 Gate width (R/L) [1-4]
137  * 45. 4 Gate width (F/B) [1-4]
138  * 46. 4 Selection of beam pattern [1-3]
139  * 47. 4 Interference removal
140  * 0:OFF, 1:ON
141  * 48. 4 KP shift [1-32]
142  * 49. 4 Sonar mode [0]
143  * 50. not used
144  * ----------------------------------------------------------
145  *
146  */
